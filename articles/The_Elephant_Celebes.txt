"The Elephant Celebes

Artist Max Ernst

Year 1921

Medium Oil on canvas

Dimensions 125.4 cm × 107.9 cm (49.4 in × 42.5 in)

Location Tate Modern, London

The Elephant Celebes (or short Celebes) is a 1921 painting by the German Dadaist and surrealist Max Ernst. It is among the most famous of Ernst's early surrealist works and "undoubtedly the first masterpiece of Surrealist painting in the de Chirico tradition." It combines the vivid dreamlike atmosphere of Surrealism with the collage aspects
