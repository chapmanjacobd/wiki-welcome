An island constructed by people

The Flevopolder in the Netherlands is 970 km2 and is the largest island formed by reclaimed land in the world.

An artificial island or man-made island is an island that has been constructed by people rather than formed by natural means. Artificial islands may vary in size from small islets reclaimed solely to support a single pillar
