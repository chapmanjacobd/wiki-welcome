For the United States law, see SPEECH Act.

In the philosophy of language and linguistics, speech act is something expressed by an individual that not only presents information, but performs an action as well. For example, the phrase "I would like the kimchi, could you please pass it to me?" is considered a speech act as it expresses the speaker's desire to acquire the kimchi, as well as presenting a request that someone pass the kimchi to them.
