Trans-Australian Railway

 The Trans-Australian Railway was opened in 1917 by Australia's national government as the first step in standardising the nation's railway gauges. However, the narrow gauge railways at both ends persisted until 1970, when the entire East-West rail corridor became standard gauge, and the mainland capital cities were not connected by standard gauge until 2004
