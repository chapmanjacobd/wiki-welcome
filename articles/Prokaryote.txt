A prokaryote is a unicellular organism that lacks a membrane-bound nucleus, mitochondria, or any other eukaryotic membrane-bound organelle. The word prokaryote comes from the Greek πρό (pro, 'before') and κάρυον (karyon, 'nut' or 'kernel'). Prokaryotes are divided into two domains, Archaea and Bacteria.
