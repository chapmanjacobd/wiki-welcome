The term Iranian Intermezzo or Persian renaissance represents a period in history which saw the rise of various native Persian Muslim dynasties in the Iranian plateau after the Arab invasion of Iran in the 7th century and the fall of Sasanian Empire. This term is noteworthy since it was an interlude between the decline of Abbāsid Arab rule and power and the "Sunni Revival" with the emergence of the Seljuq Turks in the 11th century.
