Umami (/uːˈmɑːmi/, from Japanese: 旨味 ) or savory taste is one of the five basic tastes (together with sweetness, sourness, bitterness, and saltiness). It has been described as savory and is characteristic of broths and cooked meats.

People taste umami through taste receptors that typically respond to glutamates, which are widely present in meat broths and fermented product
