Stanislas-Marie Maillard, sketch by Georges-François-Marie Gabriel, Paris, Musée Carnavalet.

Stanislas-Marie Maillard (December 11, 1763 – April 11, 1794) was a captain of the Bastille Volunteers. As a national guardsman, he participated in the attack on the Bastille, being the first revolutionary to get into the fortress, and also accompanied the women who marched to Versailles on October 5, 1789. Maillard testified in court to the events at Versailles.
