Vasily Andreyevich Zhukovsky (Russian: Василий Андреевич Жуковский, romanized: Vasiliy Andreyevich Zhukovskiy) was the foremost Russian poet of the 1810s and a leading figure in Russian literature in the first half of the 19th century. He held a high position at the Romanov court as tutor to the Grand Duchess Alexandra
