A howitzer /ˈhaʊ.ɪtsər/ is a type of artillery piece characterized by a short barrel and the use of small propellant charges to propel projectiles over high trajectories, with a steep angle of descent.

Pre-World War I 280 mm howitzer battery at Charlottenlund Fort, Denmark.
