"Penalty of exile from the ancient Greek city of Syracuse

In ancient Syracuse, Sicily, petalism was a form of banishment similar to ostracism (Ancient Greek: ὀστρακισμός) in Athens. In a special vote, citizens wrote on leaves (πέτᾰλᾰ "leaves") the names of those they wished to banish from public life. In Athens, names were written on ostraka "shells, potsherds". A certain number of such votes could send the victim into exile. The Greek term petalismós (πεταλισμός)
