Senet Senet gaming board inscribed for Amenhotep III with separate sliding drawer, c. 1390–1353 BCE

Senet (or senat) is a board game from ancient Egypt, whose original rules are the subject of conjecture. The oldest hieroglyph resembling a senet game dates to around 3100 BCE. The full name of the game in Egyptian is thought to have been zn.t n.t ḥˁb, meaning the "game of passing".
