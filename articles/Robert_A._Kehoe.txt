Kehoe in the 1930s

Robert A. Kehoe (/ˈkiːhoʊ/; November 18, 1893 – November 24, 1992) was an American toxicologist and a leader in occupational health. Kehoe was the foremost medical advocate for the use of tetraethyllead as an additive in gasoline.
