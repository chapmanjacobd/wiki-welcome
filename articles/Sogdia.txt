Sogdia

Sogdiana, c. 300 BC, then under the Seleucid Empire, one of the successor states to the empire created by Alexander the Great

Languages
 Sogdian language

Religions
 Zoroastrianism, Buddhism, Manichaeism, Nestorian Christianity

Capitals
 Samarkand, Bukhara, Khujand, Kesh

Area
 Between the Amu Darya and the Syr Darya

Existed
 6th century BC to 11th century AD

Currency
 Imitations of Sassanian coins and Chinese cash coins as well as "hybrids" of both.
