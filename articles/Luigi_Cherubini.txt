Luigi Cherubini (/ˌkɛrʊˈbiːni/ KERR-uu-BEE-nee, Italian: ; 8 or 14 September 1760 – 15 March 1842) was an Italian Classical and Romantic  composer. His most significant compositions are operas and sacred music. Beethoven regarded Cherubini as the greatest of his contemporaries. His operas were heavily praised and interpreted by Rossini.
