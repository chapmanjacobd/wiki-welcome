A 1611 woodcut of Josquin des Prez, copied from a now-lost oil painting done during his lifetime

Josquin des Prez (French: ; c. 1450/1455 – 27 August 1521), often referred to simply as Josquin, was a French composer of the Renaissance. He was the most famous European composer between Guillaume Dufay and Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina, and is usually considered to be the central figure of the Franco-Flemish School.
