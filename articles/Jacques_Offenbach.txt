Jacques Offenbach (/ˈɒfənbɑːx/, also US: /ˈɔːf-/, French: , German:  (listen); 20 June 1819 – 5 October 1880) was a German-born French composer, cellist and impresario of the Romantic period. He is remembered for his nearly 100 operettas of the 1850s to the 1870s, and his uncompleted opera The Tales of Hoffmann.
