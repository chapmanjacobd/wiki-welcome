Mori in her youth, around 1920s

Mari Mori (森 茉莉, Mori Mari, 7 January 1903 – 6 June 1987) was a Japanese author. New York University Professor Keith Vincent has called her a "Japanese Electra", referring to the Electra complex counterpart put forth by Carl Jung to Sigmund Freud's Oedipal complex.

Early life and family

Mari Mori was born in Hongō, Tokyo. Her father was novelist Mori Ōgai.
