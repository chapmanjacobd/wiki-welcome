Alban Maria Johannes Berg (/ˈɑːlbɑːn ˈbɛərɡ/; German: ; February 9, 1885 – December 24, 1935) was an Austrian composer of the Second Viennese School. His compositional style combined Romantic lyricism with the twelve-tone technique.
