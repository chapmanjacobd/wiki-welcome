Schist specimen showing the characteristic "scaly" schistose texture, caused by platy micas

Schist (pronounced /ʃɪst/ SHIST) is a medium-grade metamorphic rock formed from mudstone or shale. Schist has medium to large, flat, sheet-like grains in a preferred orientation (nearby grains are roughly parallel). It is defined by having more than 50% platy and elongated minerals (such as micas or talc), often finely interleaved with quartz and feld
