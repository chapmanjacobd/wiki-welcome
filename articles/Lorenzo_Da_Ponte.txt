Lorenzo Da Ponte

Engraving by Michele Pekenino after Nathaniel Rogers

Lorenzo Da Ponte (born Emanuele Conegliano 10 March 1749 – 17 August 1838) was an Italian, later American opera librettist, poet and Roman Catholic priest. He wrote the libretti for 28 operas by 11 composers, including three of Mozart's most celebrated operas, Don Giovanni, The Marriage of Figaro and Così fan tutte.
