Sebastiano Serlio was the first to canonize the five Classical orders (Tuscan, Ionic, Doric, Corinthian and Composite), in a prime example of classical architectural theory.

Classical architecture usually denotes architecture which is more or less consciously derived from the principles of Greek and Roman architecture of classical antiquity, or sometimes even more specifically, from the works of the Roman architect Vitruvius.
