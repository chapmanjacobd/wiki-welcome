Navigation using astronomical objects to determine position

A diagram of a nautical sextant, a tool used in celestial navigation

Celestial navigation, also known as astronavigation, is the ancient and modern practice of position fixing that enables a navigator to transition through a space without having to rely on estimated calculations, or dead reckoning, to know their position.
