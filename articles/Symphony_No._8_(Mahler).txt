Munich, September 1910: Final rehearsal for the world premiere of Mahler's Symphony No. 8 in the Neue Musik-Festhalle

The Symphony No. 8 in E-flat major by Gustav Mahler is one of the largest-scale choral works in the classical concert repertoire. Because it requires huge instrumental and vocal forces it is frequently called the "Symphony of a Thousand", although the work is normally presented with far fewer than a thousand performers and the composer did not sanction that name.
