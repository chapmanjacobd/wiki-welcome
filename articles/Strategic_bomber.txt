Contemporary U.S. Air Force strategic bombers, top to bottom: the B-52 Stratofortress, B-1 Lancer and B-2 Spirit.

A strategic bomber is a medium- to long-range penetration bomber aircraft designed to drop large amounts of air-to-ground weaponry onto a distant target for the purposes of debilitating the enemy's capacity to wage war.
