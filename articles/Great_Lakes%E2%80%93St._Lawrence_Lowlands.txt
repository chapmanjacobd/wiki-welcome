"A physiographic region in Canada

The Great Lakes–St. Lawrence Lowlands, or simply St. Lawrence Lowlands, is a physiographic region of Canada that comprises a section of southern Ontario bounded on the north by the Canadian Shield and by three of the Great Lakes—Lake Huron, Lake Erie, and Lake Ontario—and extends along the Saint Lawrence River to the Strait of Belle Isle and the Atlantic Ocean.
