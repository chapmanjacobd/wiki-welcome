For other uses, see Girdle (disambiguation).

A Christian (Anglican) priest wearing a white girdle around his waist to hold his alb and stole in place.

A belt is called a girdle if it is worn as part of Christian liturgical vestments, or in certain historical, literary or sports contexts. 

Girdles are used to close a cassock of Catholic priests or in many other Christian denominations, including the Anglican Communion, Methodist Church and Lutheran Church.
