Ahmed ‘Urabi (1900)

Ahmed ʻUrabi (; Arabic: أحمد عرابى ; 1839 – 21 September 1911), also known as Ahmed Ourabi or Orabi Pasha, was an Egyptian nationalist and an officer of the Egyptian army. The first political and military leader in Egypt to rise from the fellahin, ʻUrabi participated in an 1879 mutiny that developed into the Urabi Revolt against the Anglo-French dominated administration of Khedive Tewfik.
