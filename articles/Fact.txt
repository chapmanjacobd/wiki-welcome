Statement that is consistent with reality or can be proven with evidence

For other uses, see Fact (disambiguation).

A fact is an occurrence in the real world. For example, "This sentence contains words." is a linguistic fact, and "The sun is a star." is an astronomical fact. Further, "Abraham Lincoln was the 16th President of the United States." and "Abraham Lincoln was assassinated." are also both facts, of history. Generally speaking, facts are independent of belief.
