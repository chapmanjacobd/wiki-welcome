Tripod fish rest on the ocean bottom using three spines originating from the fins.

Tripedalism (from the Latin tri = three + ped = foot) is locomotion by the use of three legs. It has been conjectured that parrots (Psittaciformes) display tripedalism during climbing gaits, though this has not yet been documented thoroughly in scientific literature.
