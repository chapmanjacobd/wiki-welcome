Type of dry down-slope wind occurring near mountains

The causes of the foehn effect in the lee of mountains. Adapted from.

A föhn or foehn (UK: /fɜːn/, US: /feɪn/) is a type of dry, warm, down-slope wind that occurs in the lee (downwind side) of a mountain range. See alpine climate.

Föhn can be initiated when deep low-pressure systems move into Europe, drawing moist Mediterranean air over the Alps.
