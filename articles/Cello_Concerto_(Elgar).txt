Elgar and Beatrice Harrison making an early recording of the concerto (1920). Note the acoustic recording horns.

Edward Elgar's Cello Concerto in E minor, Op. 85, his last notable work, is a cornerstone of the solo cello repertoire. Elgar composed it in the aftermath of the First World War, when his music had already gone out of fashion with the concert-going public.
