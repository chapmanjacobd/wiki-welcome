Abu Tammam

Abū Tammām (أبو تمام), full name Ḥabīb ibn Aws al-Ṭā’ī (حبيب بن أوس الطائي); (ca. 796/807 - 850) was a Syrian poet and Muslim convert born to Christian parents. He is best known in literature by his 9th-century compilation of early poems known as the Hamasah, considered one of the greatest anthologies of Arabic literature ever written. Hamasah contained 10 books of poems, with 884 poems in total.
