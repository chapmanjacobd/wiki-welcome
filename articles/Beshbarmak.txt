Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak (Kyrgyz: бешбармак, Russian: бешбармак, Bashkir: бишбармаҡ, Tatar: бишбармак — "five finger") is the national dish among nomadic Turkic peoples in Central Asia and Russia. It is also known as naryn in Xinjiang, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan, as turama or dograma in Karakalpakstan, North Caucasus and Turkmenistan, and as kullama in Bashkortostan and Tatarstan.
